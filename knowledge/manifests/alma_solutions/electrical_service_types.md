# Concept: Electrical Service Types

## Overview
This document captures the domain knowledge about electrical service upgrade types relevant to Beh Brothers Electric operations.

## Service Upgrade Types

### 200A Overhead Service
**Description**: Standard residential upgrade from existing service to 200 amp capacity via overhead lines.

**Components**:
| Item | Qty | Purpose |
|------|-----|---------|
| 200A Meter Socket | 1 | Ringless/Horn Bypass |
| 200A Main Breaker Panel | 1 | If applicable |
| 4/0 Aluminum SE Cable | 40' | Service entrance cable |
| 2" Rigid Mast Kit | 1 | Hub, Weatherhead, Roof Jack |
| Ground Rods & Clamps | 2 | Grounding system |
| #4 Bare Copper (GEC) | 50' | Grounding electrode conductor |
| Service Wedge Clamps | 2 | Wire connections |

**Estimated**: 2 workers × 8 hours = 16 man-hours

### 200A Underground Service
**Description**: Service upgrade via underground conduit run.

**Components**:
| Item | Qty | Purpose |
|------|-----|---------|
| 200A Meter Socket (Side Bus) | 1 | Underground configuration |
| 2" PVC Expansion Joint | 1 | Thermal expansion |
| 2" Schedule 80 PVC Stick | 1 | Underground conduit |
| Ground Rods & Clamps | 2 | Grounding |
| #4 Bare Copper | 50' | GEC |

**Estimated**: 2 workers × 6 hours = 12 man-hours

### 100A Service Upgrade
**Description**: Standard 100 amp service upgrade.

**Estimated**: 2 workers × 6 hours = 12 man-hours

## Coordination Process
1. Client contacts utility to open work order
2. Utility schedules disconnect/reconnect
3. Beh Brothers coordinates crew for same time slot
4. Complete electrical work during outage window
5. Utility reconnects and inspects
